To add text or edit a page in Wikisocion simply press the "edit" tab of the page or the "edit" link of the page section you would like to work on. Here you can type your text, adding a few codes here and there for internal and external links, images (especially the socionic symbols), and section headings. The software (Mediawiki) for Wikisocion relies on a simple, but unique set of codes that may take a little while to master. You can get by fine without them, however, by simply typing in your text. Someone more experienced is sure to come along and make the necessary modifications. To try out some of the wiki codes, use our sandbox.

For more extensive help than is available here, see the section at the bottom of the page.

Socionics symbols

Inserting a socionics symbol is a cinch! Just use the following codes. For simplicity's sake, Viktor Gulenko's abbreviations have been used in the file names.

  • {{I}}
  • {{T}}
  • {{F}}
  • {{S}}
  • {{P}}
  • {{L}}
  • {{E}}
  • {{R}}

Common necessities

  • italics: ''text''
  • boldface: '''text'''
  • internal link: [[page name]]
  • internal link that differs from the word in the text: [[page name|text]]
  • external link: [url] or [url text]
  • bulleted list: * at the beginning of each line
  • numbered list: # at the beginning of each line
  • indent: : at the beginning of the paragraph to be indented. This is used in talk pages to indicate a response; each colon adds one level of indentation.
  • redirect: type #REDIRECT [[new title]] at the beginning of the page
  • redirect to a section of a page: same as previous, but with #REDIRECT [[new title#title of section]]
  • table: see meta
  • image: [[Image:{name}|{type}|{location}|{size}|{caption}]] (for more see meta)
  • long dash (—): &mdash;
  • block quote (indented on both sides): <blockquote>text</blockquote>
  • "code" typeface (for examples of wiki syntax): <code>text</code>

To post your user info and the current time after comments on talk pages, type ~~~~ after your comment or use the "signature" button (2nd from the right above edit fields)

Page notices

Here is a list of notices that can be inserted at the tops of pages or sections of pages:

  • {{inaccuracies}} — preview
  • {{incomplete}} — preview
  • {{good}} — preview
  • {{needs work}} — preview
  • {{review}} — preview

Wikisocion policies

At the moment anyone can edit the pages of Wikisocion, even unregistered users. This will continue until vandalism becomes an issue, if it ever does. Nonetheless, we strongly recommend that people register before contributing material (as opposed to simply editing typos) in order to build up trust among the community of editors.

Basic principles of Wikisocion are objectivity and conscientiousness, or good will. Users who disregard these considerations will be blocked.
